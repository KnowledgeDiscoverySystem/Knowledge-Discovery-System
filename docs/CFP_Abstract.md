# Conference Proposal Abstract
## Deterministic Reconstruction for Modernizing Safety‑Critical Legacy Systems
Modernization of legacy, safety‑critical systems has remained one of the most persistent and expensive challenges in aerospace, medical, defense, and industrial domains. Traditional approaches rely heavily on tribal knowledge, manual reverse engineering, and probabilistic interpretation — methods that introduce ambiguity, inflate risk, and fail to produce certification‑grade artifacts. This talk presents **KDS**, a deterministic reconstruction engine that transforms raw assembly code into complete, auditable engineering documentation, including Data Flow Diagrams, System Requirements Specifications, Software Design Documents, and full traceability matrices. Unlike AI‑assisted tools that infer or approximate system behavior, **KDS** derives every artifact directly from the assembly, ensuring that modernization efforts begin with verifiable truth rather than speculation. The session will detail the underlying methodology, demonstrate how deterministic reconstruction eliminates modernization uncertainty, and show how organizations can leverage **KDS** to accelerate re‑engineering, reduce certification burden, and restore confidence in legacy systems once considered too risky or opaque to modernize. Attendees will gain a clear understanding of how deterministic analysis reshapes the modernization landscape and why the future of critical‑system engineering depends on abandoning nondeterministic methods in favor of forensic, traceable reconstruction.
